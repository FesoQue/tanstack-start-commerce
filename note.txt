A. For multiple dynamic route segments, you can structure your file like this:
- `$postId.$reviewId.tsx`

Here, `postId` and `reviewId` are the dynamic values (route params).


B. You only reach for something like $postId.$reviewId.index.tsx when that route has children/siblings at the same level and you need a “default” page for /post/:postId/:reviewId.

- If you just need /post/:postId/:reviewId (and nothing nested under it):

Use $postId.$reviewId.tsx and you’re done.

- If you want /post/:postId/:reviewId and also things like /post/:postId/:reviewId/edit or /post/:postId/:reviewId/settings:

Then you’d do:

- $postId.$reviewId.index.tsx → handles /post/:postId/:reviewId
- $postId.$reviewId.edit.tsx → handles /post/:postId/:reviewId/edit
- $postId.$reviewId.settings.tsx → handles /post/:postId/:reviewId/settings

So: .index is for “this is the base/default page at this dynamic level, and I also have other routes hanging off the same level.”
